Probably Palaemon considered the preposition oxytone because he wished
to distinguish it in pronunciation from the adverb. Since the preposition
was only an adverb with a special use, he thought that the adverb retained
the regular initial acuent, while the preposition‚ being of later development,
was affected by this accentual change.

In this passage, Quintilian follows Cioero closely, as he frequently does
in matters of accent and rhythm. Cf. Cicero, Orator, XVHI, 58.
